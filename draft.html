Profile

Mukon Mochizuki・J.D. Andrade

A native of Illinois, J.D. began his musical journey in classical violin at the age of 9, eventually focusing on chamber music through high school and university. He first started taiko as a member of Gendo Taiko Gendo Taiko Brown University | Providence, RI (U.S.A.) Collegiate taiko group founded in 2005 by Raiki Machida and Joshua Goldner with roots in Miyagi prefecture’s Wadaiko Gensō. Visit Website at Brown University in 2006, and in 2012, he moved to Honolulu to study with Kenny Endo Kenny Endo Taiko Center of the Pacific | Honolulu, HI (U.S.A.) Esteemed taiko artist and the first non-Japanese national to get a stage name in hōgaku bayashi. Visit Website after being awarded the Taiko Center of the Pacific (TCP) Fellowship.

Upon moving to Japan in 2014, J.D. began playing taiko professionally with Taiko Project Indra, where he began his journey into the traditional performing arts. In addition to taiko, J.D. studies matsuri-bayashi (festival music) under Kyosuke Suzuki and kabuki-bayashi (traditional Japanese drumming) under Saburo Mochizuki.

In 2019, J.D. became the second foreigner, after Kenny Endo, to be awarded a stage name, or natori, in kabuki-bayashi, granted by Sakichi Mochizuki IV, headmaster of the Mochizuki School’s Morishita division. His stage name is Mukon Mochizuki (望月武懇).

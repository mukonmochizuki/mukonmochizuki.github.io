Profile

Mukon Mochizuki・J.D. Andrade

A native of Illinois, J.D. began his musical journey in classical violin at the age of 9, eventually focusing on chamber music through high school and university. He first started taiko as a member of Gendo Taiko Gendo Taiko Providence, RI (U.S.A.) Collegiate taiko group founded in 2005 by Raiki Machida and Joshua Goldner with roots in Miyagi prefecture’s Wadaiko Gensō. Visit Website at Brown University in 2006, and in 2012, he moved to Honolulu to study with Kenny Endo Kenny Endo Honolulu, HI (U.S.A.) Esteemed taiko artist and the first non-Japanese national to get a stage name in hōgaku bayashi. Visit Website after being awarded the Taiko Center of the Pacific Taiko Center of the Pacific Honolulu, HI (U.S.A.) Taiko school founded in 1994 by Kenny and Chizuko Endo with roots in the Sukeroku style of drumming from Tokyo, Japan. Visit Website (TCP) Fellowship.

Upon moving to Japan in 2014, J.D. began playing taiko professionally with Taiko Project Indra, where he began his journey into the traditional performing arts. In addition to taiko, J.D. studies matsuri-bayashi (festival music) under Kyosuke Suzuki Kyosuke Suzuki Tokyo, Japan One of the leading practitioners of Tokyo festival music, Japanese lion dance, and kagura. Wakayama Shachū holds the 無形文化財 (Intangible Cultural Property) designation for Edo Sato Kagura. Visit Website and kabuki-bayashi (traditional Japanese drumming) under Saburo Mochizuki Saburo Mochizuki Tokyo, Japan A prominent performer and teacher of hōgaku bayashi who studied with Bokusei IV, former Living National Treasure. He holds a 無形文化財 (Intangible Cultural Property) designation for nagauta. .

In 2019, J.D. became the second foreigner, after Kenny Endo, to be awarded a stage name, or natori, in kabuki-bayashi, granted by Sakichi Mochizuki IV, headmaster of the Mochizuki School’s Morishita division. His stage name is Mukon Mochizuki (望月武懇).

In 2022, J.D. had the pleasure of being featured as a kabuki-bayashi artist on TV Tokyo’s “Sekai! Nippon ni ikitai hito ōendan.” This experience propelled him to begin sharing his passion for kabuki-bayashi with the expat community in Japan, as well as the rest of the world.

In 2023, he left Indra to focus his career entirely on classical music and ohayashi.

In 2024, J.D. founded Taiko Unit Tsuzumiboshi, a Tokyo-based ensemble fusing taiko, hōgaku-bayashi, and matsuri-bayashi.

In August 2025, he participated in “Ma vs. Groove,” Kenny Endo’s 50th Anniversary Celebration in Honolulu, Hawai’i as a performer, workshop leader, interpreter, and organizer, collaborating with artists such as Sho’on Shibata Sho’on Shibata Honolulu, HI (U.S.A.)
